SIR model with multiple cities

The parameters are:
- \(n\), the number of cities;
- \(S_i(t)\), the fraction of susceptible people in city \(i\) at time \(t\);
- \(I_i(t)\), the fraction of infected people in city \(i\) at time \(t\);
- \(R_i(t)\), the fraction of removed people in city \(i\) at time \(t\);
- \(p_{ij}\), the probability that someone in city \(i\) get in touch with someone from city \(j\);
- \(\beta_i\), the transmission rate in city \(i\);
- \(\gamma_i\), the removal rate in city \(i\);

The equations are: $$ \frac{dS_i}{dt} = -\beta_iS_i \sum_{j=1}^n I_jp_{ij} $$ $$ \frac{dI_i}{dt} = \beta_iS_i \sum_{j=1}^n I_jp_{ij} - \gamma_iI_i$$ $$ \frac{dR_i}{dt} = \gamma_iI_i$$ $$ S_i(0) \in (0,1), I_i(0) = 1-S_i(0), R_i(0)=0\forall i=1,\cdots,n$$ And the probabilities \( p_{ij} \) are obtained by the parameters \(t_i\) and the sizes \(s_i\) of each city using the following rule: $$ p_{ij} = t_i t_j \frac{ s_j }{ \sum_{k=1}^n s_k }\text{ if }i\neq j \text{ and } p_{ii} = 1 - \sum_{j\neq i} p_{ij}$$
